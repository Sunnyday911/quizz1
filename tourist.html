Place must to go

Here are 4 best places to go while you went to Sidoarjo.

Lumpur Lapindo

Lumpur Lapindo is a mud volcano located in Sidoarjo, East Java, Indonesia. It has been continuously erupting hot mud since 2006, creating a vast area covered in mud. This phenomenon is a result of geological activity beneath the Earth’s surface, where mud and gases are expelled through a vent, forming a unique and ever-changing landscape. The site has become a point of interest for its unusual and dramatic appearance.

Candi pari

Candi Pari is a 14th-century Hindu temple located in the village of Candi Pari, Porong, Sidoarjo, East Java. This red brick structure stands as a testament to the architectural prowess of the Majapahit era. The temple is characterized by its square base and intricate carvings, which reflect the cultural and religious influences of the time. Its serene and historical ambiance makes it a fascinating destination for those interested in ancient architecture and Indonesian history. The temple’s proximity to the Lumpur Lapindo site adds to its appeal, offering visitors a chance to explore both natural and historical wonders in one trip.

Indah Bordir

Indah Bordir is a well-known store in Sidoarjo, East Java, specializing in high-quality embroidered clothing. They offer a wide range of traditional Indonesian garments, including kebaya, batik, and various Muslim fashion items. The intricate embroidery work on their products showcases the rich cultural heritage and craftsmanship of Indonesia. Indah Bordir is popular for its elegant designs and attention to detail, making it a favorite destination for those looking to purchase beautifully crafted traditional attire.

Monumen Jayandaru

Monumen Jayandaru is a prominent landmark in Sidoarjo, East Java, symbolizing the prosperity and cultural heritage of the region. Standing at 23 meters tall, this monument features sculptures of shrimp and milkfish, which are significant to the local economy and culture. The design, created by Balinese artist I Wayan Winten, includes a fountain and surrounding statues representing the people of Sidoarjo, such as farmers, fishermen, and traders12. The name “Jayandaru” combines “Jaya,” meaning victory, and “Ndaru,” meaning divine revelation, reflecting the aspirations and achievements of the Sidoarjo community2. This monument, located in the heart of Sidoarjo, offers a picturesque spot for visitors to learn about the local culture and enjoy a leisurely stroll.
